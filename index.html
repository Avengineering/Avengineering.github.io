what we do

We build.
What we do
HOME | Projects | Get involved | What we are | Officers

Avengineering revolves around projects. Our members play an active role in the direction of the club and we welcome your input and collaboration. Team leaders will be appointed for every competition or project based on experience, availability, and interest. If you have an idea for a competition or a project that you'd like us to collaborate on, get our attention. If you have an idea for a cool hands-on demonstration, we'll give you the floor. You got into UCLA because someone saw the potential in you to do great things, why not start now?

In addition to projects, we are forming a partnership with The Exploratory in Culver City. In exchange for contributing to their program, by either helping them teach kids, by filling out their manpower and helping around the shop, they have offered to open up their space to us for free when they aren't hosting scheduled lessons, if we can make their workshop a focal point for tinkering in LA.

As part of our partnership with The Exploratory, we will have the opportunity to volunteer and have access to the resources at the maker space:

  • MakerBot 3D printer
  • Computer lab
  • Wookworking shop
  • Arduino boards
  • Lego Robotics
  • Sewing station
  • And drawers full of spare electronic parts

Mechanical Spider
(Current)

This is Avengineering's current project for Fall 2014. We plan, at the very least, to construct a 3D printed mechanical spider that utilizes the Klann linkage for its legs. The spider will be controlled by an Arduino, which in turn will provide interactivity through bluetooh.

3D Printing

One of us personally owns a 3D printer, which plays a large part on our projects. We also sometimes have collaborations such as printing wind tunnel models for the UCLA Supermileage club. Occasionally we do some fundraising and demos on Bruin Plaza so come check us out! But if you are interested in 3D printing you can contact us at any time using the email in the get involved section.

CAD Workshop

In Fall of 2013, our first quarter, Avengineering hosted a CAD workshop with the SolidWorks software with a focus on creating models for 3D printing.

Arduino Workshop

As a follow-up to our CAD Workshop, Avengineering hosted an Arduino work shop in the Winter 2014 quarter. We introduced basic coding for the Arduino, how to output and read signals, and control flow for students new to both programming and the DIY scene.

3D Printed Arduino Robots

Our CAD workshop and Arduino workshop culminated in this project for the Spring 2014 quarter where we designed 3D printed, servo driven, Arduino controlled robots, such as drawing robots or rubber band launchers.

The Exploratory

In Fall of 2013, Avengineering also took many trips to The Exploratory, a "makerspace" in Culver City to volunteer and contribute to teaching chidren basic engineering concepts. Shown here is some of our members constructing a chair out of rolled up newspapers as an example for the children who will be trying the same challenge.

TBP Rube Goldberg
(Pre-Founding)

In Winter Quarter 2013, Avengineering participated in TBP's Rube Goldberg machine. This event resulted in the formation of the name "Avengineering."

TBP Cardboard Boat Race
(Pre-Founding)

In Spring Quarter 2013, Avengineering placed 2nd in TBP's Cardboard Boat race. Our boat did not sink until we loaded 3 people into it after multiple circuits around the pool.

About our community
What we are
HOME | Officers | Get involved | What we do | Projects

Avengineering is a new maker club dedicated to tinkering, giving students a jumping off point to learn new skills, and collaborating on a series of large-scale projects.

We are for the tinkerers who have fun building. Avengineering is for anyone with a drive to make something awesome—anyone handy with a toolbox, a paintbrush, or an idea. We want to expand beyond the theories taught in lecture and bridge the gap between the Sciences and the Arts to keep the passion for learning alive.
